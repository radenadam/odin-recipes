Sate Ayam

Description

Sate Ayam, or Indonesian Chicken Satay, is a beloved street food and culinary classic featuring skewers of marinated chicken grilled to perfection. The chicken is typically marinated in a blend of spices like turmeric, garlic, coriander, and cumin, giving it a rich, aromatic flavor. Once grilled, the satay is served with a creamy, savory peanut sauce made from ground peanuts, kecap manis (sweet soy sauce), lime juice, and spices. Often accompanied by steamed rice cakes (ketupat) or sliced cucumbers and onions, Sate Ayam is a flavorful and satisfying dish that highlights Indonesia's vibrant culinary traditions.

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken thighs, thinly sliced into strips
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (for grilling)
  • Bamboo skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes
  • Marinade:
    • 1 tablespoon turmeric powder (or fresh turmeric, grated)
    • 3 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 2 tablespoons kecap manis (sweet soy sauce)
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
    • 1 teaspoon sugar
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Peanut Sauce:
    • 200g roasted peanuts, finely ground
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 2 red chilies, chopped (adjust to taste)
    • 2 tablespoons kecap manis (sweet soy sauce)
    • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste (or lime juice)
    • 1/2 cup water (adjust for consistency)
    • 1 teaspoon sugar
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Garnish/Serving:
    • Sliced cucumbers
    • Sliced shallots or onions
    • Steamed rice cakes (ketupat), optional
